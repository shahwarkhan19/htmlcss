Who are the Pashtuns?

For those not in the know, Pashtuns—also known as “Pathans” and “Pakhtuns”—are an ethnic group of people found throughout Afghanistan and Pakistan.They live in a strict tribal system.Pathans are faithful Muslims. Their typical martial and religious character has been moulded by their heroes, like Khushal Khan Khattak, the warrior poet and Rehman Baba , a preacher and also a poet of Pushto language.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is the land of hardworking and devoted people who lives in the western part of Pakistan. Most Pashtuns are business-minded people and they have taken hold of different businesses throughout Pakistan. The hardworking Pashtuns are not just in business rather in different fields. They are good at studies and hold a prominent position at every level. Pashtuns are famous for their hospitality, and it shows their nature of love and cares towards others. They are also well known for their variety of traditional foods and offer many food items to their guests. It is not about their guests but they themselves have a craze for eating good foodies. Pashtun cuisines are highly influenced by rich cultural surroundings like Afghanistan. Because Afghanistan has a handsome number of Pashtun tribes. The cuisines are a mixture of Pakistani and Afghani items, and they are largely based on cereals like wheat, maize, barley, and rice. The distinct characteristics of Pashtuns, welcome everyone with cordial hospitality at their homes and serve them with delicious foods of their culture.
